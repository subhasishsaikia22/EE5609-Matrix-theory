\section{Problem}
Show that the lines with the directional vectors 
\begin{align}
    \myvec{12 \\-3 \\-4},\quad \myvec{4 \\12 \\3}  \quad and \quad \myvec{3 \\-4 \\12}
\end{align} 
are mutually perpendicular.
\section{Explanation}
When two vectors are perpendicular to each other,their inner product is zero.
 Let 
 \begin{align}
     \vec{A}= \myvec{12 \\-3 \\-4}\\
     \vec{B}= \myvec{4 \\12 \\3}\\
     \vec{C}=\myvec{3 \\-4 \\12} 
 \end{align}
 The inner product of $\vec{A}$ and $\vec{B}$ is
\begin{align}
\vec{A}^T\vec{B}=(12\cdot4)+(-3\cdot12)+(-4\cdot3)=0
\end{align}
The inner product of $\vec{B}$ and $\vec{C}$ is
\begin{align}
\vec{B}^T\vec{C}=(4\cdot3)+(12\cdot-4)+(3\cdot12)=0
\end{align}
The inner product of $\vec{C}$ and $\vec{A}$ is
\begin{align}
\vec{C}^T\vec{A}=(3\cdot12)+(-4\cdot-3)+(12\cdot-4)=0
\end{align}
Hence, the three lines with the given directional vectors are mutually perpendicular.
